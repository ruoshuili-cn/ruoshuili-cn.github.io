Corporate Renewable Path -- Market Tools and Criticisms

June 18, 2020

Business interests in renewable energy investment have surged from the last decade. Many corporations around the world have adopted elaborate measures to meet ambitious sustainability targets. Depending on different corporation goals, external market rules and operation models, there are various market tools available for a company to arrange for a successful emissions management plan. However, some environmentalists argue that details regarding their actual behaviors and procurement progress released publicly are not supportive enough for people to fully understand their sustainability claims. In this report, I will briefly introduce the variations in each approach and state how specific sustainability method has been condemned inefficient.

Development of Global Oil Giant’s Renewable Business [updated in August, 2020]

May 21, 2020

As the impact of COVID-19 pandemic rages all over the world, global oil demand has slumped to a 25-year low. The following production disputes between Saudi Arabia and Russia deepened the supply-side crisis and led to a historically low oil price of $24.88 /barrel in Mar.18th. However, even when all oil companies are cutting costs to improve the financial situation, their renewable energy businesses are mostly spared untouched. In this long report, I’m interested in seeing how those fossil fuel companies have embraced clean energy projects for the last decades and whether they can transit deeper toward carbon-free model in the darkest moment of the oil industry.

Nikola V.S. Tesla: Hydrogen Fuel Cells or Battery?

July 11, 2020

Nikola Motor, a hydrogen and electric truck startup, has been a rising star in the market. After completing its reverse merger with public-traded company VectoIQ Acquisition Corp, Nikola Motor surged at most 130% in the first week of trading, even when no products are delivered in the market. As a fuel-cell pioneer in the vehicle industry, Nikola has gained a market value higher than any other automakers in America except General Motors and Tesla Motor. Discussion between hydrogen and battery-backed electric vehicles also revived with the new Nasdaq business. In this report, we will discuss the following questions: In terms of technical parameters and vehicle performance, what’s the difference between Tesla’s electric semi and Nikola’s hydrogen truck? What challenges will Nikola have to address in the next few years before commercial products enter production? Is there any external influence factor the company need to be prepared? And finally, CAN Nikola replicate Tesla’s success and occupy substaintial market share in vehile industry?
